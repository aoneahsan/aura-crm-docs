# Tasks

AuraCRM keeps two kinds of action item apart, because they belong to two different people:

- **Professional tasks** — things *you* need to do (send a document, book a room, prepare an
  agenda).
- **Client homework** — things the *client* agreed to do before the next session.

## Where tasks come from

- **From a session.** When you approve an [AI proposal](/docs/features/ai-proposals), the extracted
  tasks and homework are created against the client.
- **By hand.** You can add a task or piece of homework directly on a client at any time.

## Tracking them

- Tasks have a clear status you move through as work gets done.
- Client homework you assign appears in that client's [portal](/docs/features/client-portal) once
  you publish it.
- When a client checks off a homework item in their portal, the matching task is marked done on your
  side and the change is written to the client's audit history — so both views stay in step.

## Why the split matters

Mixing your own to-dos with what a client owes you is how follow-through slips. Keeping them
separate means your task list is genuinely *your* work, and the client's portal shows them exactly
what is theirs.
